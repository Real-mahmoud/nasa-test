About the Challenge

The “Will it Rain on My Parade?” challenge is about using NASA climate and weather data to explore how rainfall and other environmental factors can affect important outdoor events.

In this project, you’ll discover how satellite-based climate datasets can be used to predict conditions such as temperature, precipitation, and wind — helping people plan their parades, celebrations, and activities with more confidence.

Our goal is to make this information accessible to everyone by combining interactive maps, search tools, and data visualizations in one platform.

How It Works

Step 1

Enter a location and a date into the search form.

Step 2

We fetch real climate data from NASA’s POWER API.

Step 3

Results are shown on an interactive map with visual charts.

Step 4

You can check if it might rain on your parade or not!

Why It Matters

Understanding climate data helps us prepare for the future. By analyzing rainfall, temperature, and weather patterns, we can make better decisions about events, agriculture, and safety.

NASA’s climate datasets are not just numbers—they are insights that help communities adapt and thrive in a changing world.
